HANA KALPAK

Philosophy PhD student

Stockholm University

 

I’m a PhD student in philosophy at Stockholm University, specialized in formal epistemology and semantics. I think and write about rational belief, rational inquiry, and the relation between the two.

I started as a PhD student in February, 2019. Before this, I got a Master’s degree in logic from University of Amsterdam in 2018, and a Bachelor’s degree in philosophy (with a minor in linguistics) from Stockholm University in 2016.

Since 2020 I am associated with the project Knowledge Resistance.

Sedan 2021 är jag del av redaktionen för Svensk Filosofi, som syftar till att synliggöra svensk forskning och utbildning i filosofi. Där har jag även skrivit om språkfilosofi.

projects

some things I’m working on

Feel free to email me for drafts or discussion.

Decision rules for imprecise Lockeans

I outline some ways to establish a utility-theoretic Lockean thesis connecting our rational beliefs to our rational imprecise credences.

Relevance and the aim of inquiry

I develop a relevance-first theory of rational inquiry, and relate it to accuracy-first theories of rational belief/credence.

Question-sensitive beliefs and question-sensitive contents

In a certain sense, our beliefs are question-sensitive. I reason about how this assumption should (not) affect our semantics of belief reports as well as our general theory of communication.

Minimal rationality of fragmented and question-sensitive beliefs

I study the logic of fragmented and question-sensitive beliefs, and use it to propose some minimal rationality constraints for agents with such beliefs.
